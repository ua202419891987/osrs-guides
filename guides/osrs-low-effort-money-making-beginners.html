💰 Money Making 🟢 Beginner Updated 2026

Why Low Effort Methods Are Perfect for Beginners

When you're new to OSRS, your stats are low and your gold reserves are thin. Grinding for high-level money makers is frustrating. Low effort methods let you build a gold cushion while you level up other skills, so you're never broke.

In 2026, the OSRS economy has made many basic resources valuable due to active player trading. Even beginner methods can earn you 50,000–200,000 GP per hour with zero risk.

Method 1: Picking Flax (Free-to-Play)

📊 Profit: ~60,000 GP/hr ⚡ Req: None 🗺️ Location: Seers' Village

Head to the flax field just south of Seers' Village. Pick flax and bank it at the nearby bank. Flax sells for around 100–130 GP each on the Grand Exchange.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Travel to Seers' Village (use lodestone or walk from Camelot)
  2. Pick a full inventory of flax (28 items)
  3. Bank at Seers' Village bank (30 seconds walk)
  4. Repeat — you can pick about 600–700 flax per hour

💡 2026 Tip: If you have level 10 Crafting, spin the flax into bow strings first — they sell for 180–220 GP each, nearly doubling your profit.

Method 2: Tanning Hides at Al Kharid

📊 Profit: ~80,000–150,000 GP/hr ⚡ Req: None (members) 🗺️ Location: Al Kharid

Buy cowhides from the Grand Exchange, tan them at Ellis the tanner in Al Kharid, and sell the leather back. The tanner charges 1 GP per hide for soft leather or 45 GP for hard leather.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Buy 1,000+ cowhides from the Grand Exchange
  2. Teleport to Al Kharid (glory amulet or walk)
  3. Talk to Ellis and tan all hides at once
  4. Sell hard leather on the GE for profit

💡 Profit margin fluctuates — always check the GE price before buying bulk. In May 2026, hard leather gives ~40–60 GP profit per hide after tanning cost.

Method 3: Grand Exchange Flipping (No Stats Needed)

📊 Profit: 100,000–500,000 GP/hr (with capital) ⚡ Req: Some starting gold 🗺️ Location: Grand Exchange

Flipping means buying items below their market value and selling above. It requires zero combat stats, just patience and market awareness.

Basic Flipping for Beginners:

  1. Start with 50,000–100,000 GP as capital
  2. Search for items with a buy/sell margin of 5% or more
  3. Buy at the lower price, wait, sell at higher price
  4. Reinvest profits to grow your capital

Good starter flip items include: cannonballs, Nature runes, and common herbs like Ranarr weed. Check our full flipping guide for detailed margins.

Method 4: Collecting Snape Grass (Members)

📊 Profit: ~100,000 GP/hr ⚡ Req: None 🗺️ Location: Hobgoblin Peninsula

Snape grass is used in Herblore and Farming, making it consistently in demand. The Hobgoblin Peninsula on the western tip of the Void Knights' Outpost is packed with snape grass spawns.

How to Get There:

  1. Take the boat to Pest Control from Port Sarim
  2. Walk west to the Hobgoblin Peninsula
  3. Collect snape grass (about 100+ per hour)
  4. Bank at the nearby chest or teleport out

💡 Each snape grass sells for about 1,000–1,200 GP in 2026, making this one of the best no-requirement methods in the game.

Method 5: Killing Cows (Classic Early Game)

📊 Profit: 40,000–70,000 GP/hr ⚡ Req: Low combat stats 🗺️ Location: Lumbridge / Burthorpe

The timeless beginner method. Cows drop cowhides (worth ~150–200 GP each) and raw beef. You can also train combat at the same time, making this method extremely efficient for new players.

Best Cow Locations in 2026:

  • Lumbridge East Farm — very close to a bank, ideal for beginners
  • Crafting Guild — requires level 40 Crafting, more cows spawns
  • Burthorpe — great for ironmen starting out

Collect every cowhide, bank regularly, and sell in bulk on the GE.

Method 6: Wines of Zamorak via Telegrab

📊 Profit: ~120,000 GP/hr ⚡ Req: Level 33 Magic 🗺️ Location: Asgarnia Ice Dungeon / Chaos Temple

Wines of Zamorak are used in Herblore and consistently sell for 3,000–4,000 GP each. Using the Telegrab spell (level 33 Magic), you can collect them without fighting the monks who protect them.

How It Works:

  1. Get to level 33 Magic (train on low-level monsters)
  2. Bring law runes and air runes for Telegrab
  3. Go to the Chaos Temple in the Wilderness (single-combat, low risk)
  4. Telegrab the wines from across the altar — monks won't attack if you don't step on the altar
  5. You can grab about 100+ per hour

⚠️ Warning: The Wilderness Chaos Temple has PK risk. Use the Asgarnia Ice Dungeon (F2P-accessible) as a safer alternative.

Pro Tips for Beginner Money Makers

  • ✅ Always check GE prices before committing to a method — prices shift daily
  • ✅ Combine training with profit — cowkilling trains combat AND makes gold
  • ✅ Don't hold onto resources — sell weekly to avoid price crashes
  • ✅ Stack your GP — once you hit 500K, you can unlock better methods
  • ✅ Ironmen should focus on skill training that yields sellable items (flax spinning, crafting)
