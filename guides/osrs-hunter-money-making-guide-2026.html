OSRS Hunter Money Making Guide 2026

Money Making Hunter Updated 2026

Hunter is one of the most lucrative skilling money-makers in OSRS. Black chinchompas in the Wilderness can net 1–3M GP per hour at high levels, while red chinchompas in the Feldip Hills offer safer but still excellent profits. This guide breaks down all the best Hunter money-making methods with profit per hour estimates, gear setups, and practical tips.

Why Hunter for Money Making?

Hunter is unusual in OSRS because high-level Hunter activities directly produce high-value stackable items (chinchompas) that are constantly in demand by players training Ranged. Unlike most skills, your Hunter level directly translates to GP per hour — higher levels let you set more traps and catch faster.

  • Black chinchompas sell for ~900–1,400 GP each (prices fluctuate with demand).
  • Red chinchompas sell for ~400–700 GP each — lower value but safer location.
  • Herbiboar gives herb XP and Herblore supplies instead of pure GP.
  • Jungle Hornets and various falconry targets provide niche mid-level GP.
  • Level 80 Hunter allows 5 traps — the highest trap count, dramatically increasing catch rate.

At level 80+ in the Wilderness, you can set 6 traps simultaneously — this is the maximum possible and is the main reason black chinchompas are so much more profitable than red ones despite lower individual value per chinchompa.

Black Chinchompas — Highest Profit

Black chinchompas are caught in level 30–32 Wilderness, north of the Lava Maze. They require level 73 Hunter and carry significant risk due to PKers who frequently target chinchompa hunters. Despite the risk, the profit potential is unmatched for a skilling activity.

Black Chinchompas Method Summary

  • Location: Wilderness (level 30–32), north-east of Lava Maze
  • Hunter Level: 73 minimum, 80+ recommended
  • Profit/hr: ~1,000,000 – 3,000,000 GP depending on level and luck
  • Risk: Very high — PKers are active; bring minimum gear
  • XP/hr: ~60,000 – 100,000 (varies with catches)
  • Traps: 5–6 (6 in Wilderness at level 80+)

Black Chin Setup (Anti-PK)

When hunting black chins, minimise your risk by bringing as little as possible:

  1. Bring only box traps (fill your inventory — more traps = faster catching).
  2. No armour or weapons necessary — you are not in combat.
  3. Teleport out immediately if a PKer appears — use Camelot or other teleports that work below level 30 Wilderness.
  4. Consider logging out at the first sign of PKers and logging back in on a different world.
  5. Use the Wilderness Sword teleport or Ring of Dueling for quick escapes.
  6. Note: Revenant caves are nearby — expect PKers at all times.

Red Chinchompas — Safe Profit

Red chinchompas are caught in the Feldip Hills south of Yanille, a safe non-Wilderness zone. They require level 63 Hunter. While less profitable than black chins, they are significantly safer and still excellent money for mid-level players.

Red Chinchompas Method Summary

  • Location: Feldip Hills (south of Yanille), or in the private red chin area via Hard Western Provinces Diary
  • Hunter Level: 63 minimum
  • Profit/hr: ~400,000 – 800,000 GP
  • Risk: None (safe area)
  • XP/hr: ~60,000 – 100,000
  • Tip: Complete Western Provinces Hard Diary for access to the private chinchompa hunting ground (no competition)

Herbiboar — Passive Herb Income

The Herbiboar is a creature on Fossil Island that gives Herblore XP and herbs when caught. It requires level 80 Hunter and level 31 Herblore. While it does not give stackable items like chinchompas, it provides excellent passive herb income — especially valuable for Ironmen.

Herbiboar Method Summary

  • Location: Mushroom Meadow, Fossil Island
  • Requirements: 80 Hunter, 31 Herblore, Fossil Island access (post-Client of Kourend)
  • Herbs per hour: ~100–140 grimy herbs (mix of tiers)
  • GP/hr from herbs: ~300,000 – 600,000 (depends on herb tier)
  • XP/hr: ~50,000 – 60,000 Hunter XP
  • Also gives: Herblore XP when cleaning herbs

Hunter Money Making Tips

  • Always prioritise reaching level 80 Hunter — the 5th trap is a massive productivity increase.
  • Complete the Western Provinces Hard Diary for a private red chin hunting area with no competing players.
  • If black chin hunting, never bring items you are not willing to lose — PKers are very common.
  • Use a Ring of Dueling or Ardougne Cloak teleport for fast escape from black chin spots.
  • Chinchompa prices fluctuate — check the GE before starting a session and sell in batches rather than all at once.
  • The Hunter skillcape perk summons a falcon which passively helps catch implings — useful at higher levels.
  • Tracking polar kebbits at level 1 and working through the early Hunter levels quickly enables faster access to profitable methods.
