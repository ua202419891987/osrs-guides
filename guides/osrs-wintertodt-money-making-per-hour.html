OSRS Wintertodt Money Making Per Hour 2026

Money Making Minigame Firemaking Updated 2026

Wintertodt is one of the best early-to-mid game money makers in OSRS, offering consistent GP alongside Firemaking XP. The supply crates you earn contain seeds, herbs, logs, ores, and occasionally rare drops worth hundreds of thousands of GP. This guide explains how to optimise your Wintertodt runs for maximum profit per hour, including crate value calculations and a strategy for consistent high-point games.

What is Wintertodt?

Wintertodt is a Firemaking minigame boss located north of Arceuus in Great Kourend. Players work cooperatively to subdue the Wintertodt by lighting and maintaining fires around the arena. When the Wintertodt is fully subdued, all players who contributed enough points receive a supply crate proportional to the number of points they earned.

  • Requires level 50 Firemaking to participate.
  • Higher levels in Firemaking, Woodcutting, Herblore, and Construction increase points earned per action.
  • Supply crate quality scales with your skilling levels — more Herblore = better herb drops.
  • No combat required — entirely a skilling activity.
  • Can be done on an Ironman account for passive herb and supply sourcing.

Recommended Warm Clothing

Wearing warm clothing reduces the damage you take from the cold environment. Each piece reduces damage by approximately 1 per action:

  • Pyromancer outfit (full set, from Wintertodt drops) — 4 pieces, best reduction
  • Clue hunter outfit, warm gloves, or any other warm item
  • Fire cape counts as warm clothing
  • Wearing 4 warm items means almost no cold damage received

How to Earn Points Efficiently

Your supply crate count depends on points earned per game. You need a minimum of 500 points to receive a crate. Most players aim for 750–1,000 points per game to maximise crates per hour.

Points Earning Actions

  • Chopping Bruma Roots: +7 to +12 points per root (scales with Woodcutting level)
  • Fletching Bruma Roots into Kindling: +4 to +8 extra points (scales with Fletching level)
  • Lighting/Feeding Braziers: +25 to +75 points per fuel
  • Healing Pyromancers: +30 points per heal
  • Fixing Braziers: +25 points per repair

The most efficient strategy is to chop roots, fletch them into kindling (if your Fletching is 50+), then feed them to the brazier. Fletching gives extra points at no extra time cost, significantly increasing points per hour.

How to Optimise Wintertodt Runs

Optimal Wintertodt Strategy

  1. Position yourself next to a brazier at the start of each game.
  2. When the game begins, chop bruma roots until your inventory is full.
  3. If Fletching level is 50+, fletch all roots into kindling before feeding the brazier.
  4. Feed the brazier when lit — do not waste time feeding an unlit brazier.
  5. If your brazier goes out, immediately relight it with your tinderbox.
  6. Heal pyromancers when they fall to low health — earns extra points and helps the group.
  7. Eat food when the cold damage brings you below 20 HP.
  8. If using the bank chest between games, do so quickly — each second between games costs potential XP.

Rare Drops to Watch For

  • Torstol seeds — 100,000+ GP each, rare but possible from crates.
  • Magic seeds — 70,000+ GP each.
  • Dragon bones — 2,500 GP each, can appear in stacks.
  • Pyromancer outfit pieces — First time drops from subduing Wintertodt for the first time at each piece. After the full set, pieces worth ~30,000 GP each.
  • Warm gloves — Cosmetic/warm clothing, modest GE value.

Wintertodt Tips

  • Always wear 4 pieces of warm clothing — cold damage waste food, and food restores take time away from skilling.
  • High Herblore level is the single biggest factor in crate value — train Herblore before grinding Wintertodt for profit.
  • Don't forget to bank your supply crates and open them later in batches — opening a full inventory at once is faster.
  • The Phoenix pet is a very rare drop directly from the Wintertodt — not a crate drop. Very low probability but highly valuable.
  • Wintertodt also provides passive Construction XP when fixing braziers — useful for Ironmen.
  • At 50 Firemaking, XP rates are ~50,000/hr. At 99 Firemaking, rates reach ~85,000/hr from Wintertodt.
