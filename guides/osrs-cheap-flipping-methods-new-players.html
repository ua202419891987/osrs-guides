Why Flipping Works for New Players

Flipping is the process of buying items at a low price on the Grand Exchange and selling them at a higher price. For new players, it's one of the few money-making methods that requires zero skill levels, zero quest completions, and zero combat stats. All you need is a small amount of gold and patience.

The Grand Exchange operates on a supply and demand system. Players who want items immediately will pay a premium, while players who want to sell quickly will accept a discount. This creates a margin — the gap between the instant-buy and instant-sell prices — and that margin is where flippers make their profit.

Getting Started With Under 100K

If you're brand new and only have a few thousand gold, start by earning your first 50K through basic methods like killing cows for cowhides or mining clay. Once you have at least 50K, you can begin flipping effectively.

The Margin Check Process:

  1. Buy 1 of an item at 20% above market price (instabuy) — note the price it bought at
  2. Immediately sell that 1 item at 20% below market price (instasell) — note the price it sold at
  3. Subtract the buy price from the sell price to find your margin
  4. If the margin is at least 1% of the item's value, it's worth flipping
  5. Buy at the instasell price and sell at the instabuy price in bulk

Each margin check costs a small amount of GP (the difference between your test buy and sell), but it reveals exactly what profit you can expect on that item.

Cannonball Flipping Deep Dive

Cannonballs are arguably the single best item for new players to flip. They have a massive buy limit of 10,000, extremely high daily volume, and a consistent margin. Slayers and range trainers buy cannonballs in huge quantities every day, creating constant demand.

Cannonball Flip Example

Buy Price195 GP each
Sell Price202 GP each
Margin7 GP each
Profit per 10K70K GP

To flip cannonballs: do a margin check first, then place a buy offer for 10,000 at the instasell price. Once your offer fills, immediately list them at the instabuy price. The entire cycle typically takes 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on the time of day.

Key tip: cannonball prices tend to be lower on weekdays and higher on weekends when more players are slaying. Buy on Monday, sell on Saturday for maximum margin.

Nature Rune Flipping Strategy

Nature runes are another excellent budget item. With a buy limit of 25,000 and huge daily volume, they're perfect for players with 40K–80K capital. Nature runes are consumed constantly by players training Magic with High Level Alchemy, ensuring demand never drops.

The higher buy limit means you can invest more capital per flip cycle. Nature rune margins tend to widen during double XP-style events or when new content releases that requires alching.

Tips for New Flippers

  • ✅ Start with items you understand — if you use cannonballs for slayer, you already know their price behavior
  • ✅ Spread your capital — don't put all 100K into one item; split across 2–3 items
  • ✅ Check margins fresh each time — margins change daily, yesterday's profitable item might not work today
  • ❌ Don't flip expensive items with small capital — a 5K margin on a 500K item is not efficient with only 100K
  • ❌ Don't panic sell — if an item drops slightly, be patient; it usually recovers
  • ✅ Flip during peak hours — more players online means more trades and faster fills
  • ❌ Don't ignore the 4-hour buy limit — plan your flips around it
